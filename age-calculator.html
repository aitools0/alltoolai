How to Calculate a Child's Chronological Age

Many users ask, "how to calculate a child's chronological age" manually for reports. While our age calculator chronological tool does this instantly, here is the manual formula used in clinical settings:

  1. List the Dates: Write down the Date of Testing and the Date of Birth (Year, Month, Day).
  2. Subtract Days: Subtract the DOB Day from the Test Day. If the test day is smaller, borrow 30 days from the "Month" column.
  3. Subtract Months: Subtract the DOB Month from the Test Month. If the test month is smaller, borrow 12 months from the "Year" column.
  4. Subtract Years: Subtract the DOB Year from the Test Year.
  5. Result: The final numbers give you the precise chronological age required for scoring.
